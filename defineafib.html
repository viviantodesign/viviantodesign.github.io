PROJECT OVERVIEW

DEFINE AFib is the pilot clinical study hosted on the Medtronic Discovery. This study aimed to gather atrial fibrillation (AF) episode data from patient implantable cardiac monitors (ICMs) and survey data on symptoms and treatments.

Its goal was to develop an AI model capable of identifying connections between AF burden and symptoms, aiding clinicians in devising more effective treatment strategies for patients.

PROBLEM SPACE

Clinicians often struggle to identify effective treatment plans and patients must build their lives around their AF symptoms, struggling with:

   SYMPTOMS
  • Heart palpitations
  • Chest pain
  • Dizziness and lightheadedness
  • Fatigue
  • Reduced ability to exercise
  • Shortness of breath
   RISKS
  • High blood pressure
  • Diabetes
  • Heart failure
  • Various heart valve disease

Providing patients their data

Patients desire active involvement in their healthcare journey, with a key request being access to their heart device data. In the DEFINE AFib study, we addressed this by providing patients with their data to encourage ongoing engagement in survey tasks.

UX RESEARCH AND FORMATIVE TESTING

define + ideate

Early data discovery with ICM patients revealed what type of health data they currently track and what they want to see most from their heart device. This informed the first UI concepts for the data trends in DEFINE AFib.

Preliminary concept ideation helped define the types of data and UI architecture that provides a simple, familiar experience for patients to consume health information from their heart devices.

   PATIENTS LIKED
  • Concise, scannable, dashboard
  • Detailed trends
  • Simplification of complex data
  • Only key types of data shown
   PATIENTS DISLIKED
  • Extra clicks to find data trends
  • Lack of details
  • Cognitive burden when shown too many types of data
  • Leaving the app to view data (Apple Health data integration)

FINAL FEATURE UI

Through four rounds of formative testing involving over 32 patients, I developed and refined data visualization concepts, optimizing UI patterns, workflows, information architecture, and native iOS styling for a fluid and intuitive user experience.
